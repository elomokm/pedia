English version

0:00 – 0:30PROBLEM

In pediatrics, everything relies on observation. A child doesn't describe their symptoms — their parents live them, every day.

Ideally, a child is seen every 3 to 6 months. But between two consultations, 180 days of observations escape the medical system. And during the appointment, 12 minutes to recount 3 months. Parents forget, don't always speak the local language, or live in medical deserts.

The result: a pediatric rare disease takes an average of 4 years to be diagnosed.

0:30 – 1:00SOLUTION

MyOntologyNote is a multimodal, multilingual pediatric journal, prescribed by physicians. Parents can log every day — voice, text, photo, emoji, in their mother tongue.

Our AI turns each observation into a standardized clinical indicator — frequency, intensity, duration, triggers — powered by the HPO ontology.

Before each appointment, the pediatrician receives a one-page structured medical report with evolution curves. If a concerning signal appears, the physician is alerted first.

French TAM: €50 million. Natural extension into the 163 countries with an equivalent of the child health record.

Usage notes

When to use 90 sec vs 3 min

  • 90 seconds — hallway mentor chats, real elevator pitch, networking during the hackathon, backup if the 3-min demo crashes
  • 3 minutes — official pitch to judges, formal presentation, when there's time for a real live demo

Delivery tips

  • The first 10 words should come out without thinking — rehearse until automatic
  • Time each segment — if problem takes 35 sec, you're overrunning
  • Half-second pause after each key figure (4 years, 1h15, 16x) — let it land
  • Direct eye contact during the ROI numbers — never read from notes there

When to use which language

  • Official hackathon pitch — French (ANDCS × MIT Hacking Medicine Paris, majority Francophone judges)
  • Zen Chu (MIT) and international mentors — English, speak their professional language
  • LinkedIn / follow-up emails — English, for international reach
