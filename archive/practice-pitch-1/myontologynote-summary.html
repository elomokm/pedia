Project Summary

One-line pitch

A multimodal, multilingual pediatric journal, prescribed by physicians, that turns daily parental observations into structured medical reports before each consultation.

The pediatric problem

  • In pediatrics, everything relies on parental observation — children don't describe their symptoms
  • Ideally, a child is seen every 3 to 6 months by their pediatrician
  • Between two consultations, 90 to 180 days of observations escape the medical system
  • During a consultation: 12 minutes to recount 3 months
  • Result: a pediatric rare disease takes an average of 4 years to be diagnosed

Parent pain points

  • Forget half of their observations
  • Can't find the right words (especially non-French-speaking parents)
  • Live far from a physician (medical deserts)

Physician pain points

  • Reconstruct 3 months of observations in 12 minutes
  • Discover a key signal too late
  • Miss early detection (4-year diagnostic wandering for rare diseases)

The solution

  • Prescribed by the pediatrician when there's something to track (persistent cough, chronic condition, diagnostic doubt)
  • Daily multimodal logging: voice, text, photo, emoji
  • Native multilingual support: parent inputs in their mother tongue, physician reads in French
  • AI extraction turns each observation into a standardized clinical indicator (frequency, intensity, duration, triggers)
  • HPO ontology as the pivot (17,000 internationally standardized phenotypes)
  • Structured one-page medical report with evolution curves of key indicators, delivered before each appointment
  • Alert system: physician alerted first, parent can be notified non-alarmingly

Non-negotiable clinical rules

  • No diagnosis, ever
  • Neutral medical vocabulary (no "severe", "worrying", "alarming")
  • Parental attribution on every observation
  • Alerts go to the physician first, parent notified without alarmism
  • Explicit consent required for any data sharing
  • Parents: product always free
